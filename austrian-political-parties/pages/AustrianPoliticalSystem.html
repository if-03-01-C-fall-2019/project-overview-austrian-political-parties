Austrian Political System

The Austrian Constitution

The 5 Principles

  1. the democratic principle: Austria is governed by popular government
  2. the republican principle: The President is head of state
  3. the federal principle: Austria consists of 9 federal statses
  4. the rule of law principle: Everything is bound to the law
  5. the liberal principle: The basic human rights apply to everyone

General Structure

Separation of Powers

Political power in Austria is separated between 3 branches:

  1. the executive branch responsible for governing and exercising authority in Austria
  2. the legislative branch responsible for passing new laws on federal and national level
  3. the judicial branch responsible for interpretation of and judgement based on the laws

Federal States

  • Austria is a federal republic made up of 9 federal states
  • each of them has their own elected state government and governor
  • they only regulate minor subjects like alcohol control
  • they have very little power compared to the national council

Executive Branch

The Head of State - The President

  • current president: Alexander Van der Bellen (since 2016)
  • mainly ceremonial status
  • has the power to create or dismiss a cabinet in emergency situations
  • gets elected by popular vote in a nationwide general election every 6 years

The Head of Government - The Chancellor

  • current chancellor: Brigitte Bierlein (since 2019)
  • responsible for passing new nationwide laws
    • consists of 183 members, which are sent by the political parties
  • gets elected by popular vote in a nationwide general election every 5 years

Law Enforcement - The Police

  • responsible for passing new nationwide laws
  • consists of 183 members, which are sent by the political parties
  • parties get elected by popular vote in a nationwide general election every 5 years

Legislative Branch

The National Council

  • responsible for passing new nationwide laws
  • consists of 183 members, which are sent by the political parties
  • parties get elected by popular vote in a nationwide general election every 5 years

The Federal Council

  • there are 9 federal councils, 1 for each federal state
  • responsible for passing new laws and regulations on federal level like for example alcohol regulation
  • consists of 61 members, which are sent by the political parties
  • parties get elected by popular vote in a federal election every 5 years

Judicial Branch

The Court System

  • consists of two branches:
  • general courts
    • responsible for most civil and criminal cases
    • has a 4 level court hierarchy: district, regional, higher regional, and supreme
  • courts of public law
    • consists of 2 court groups:
    • the constitutional court responsible for liability claims against the republic, a province or a municipality
    • administrative courts responsible for judicial review of administrative actions
